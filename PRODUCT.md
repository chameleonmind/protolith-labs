# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two equally weighted audiences: early-stage founders and startup teams who need an expert partner to design and ship an MVP fast; and established product teams at scale-ups who need a senior engineer or small team to own and deliver a critical module.

Both groups are technically literate. They are not looking for explanations of what a consultancy does — they are evaluating capability and trust on contact. The job they are hiring for: turn a problem and a deadline into working production software.

## Product Purpose

Protolith Labs is a marketing website for a full-stack product consultancy offering MVP development, full-stack engineering, refactoring, frontend consulting, and design services.

The site's only conversion goal is a qualified contact form submission. Success looks like a potential client describing their project, timeline, and goals — and getting a thoughtful reply within a day or two.

## Brand Personality

Considered, craft-forward, calm. Expert confidence communicated through restraint and intentional detail — not through claims or social proof stacking. The site feels like the work of a senior engineer who has nothing to prove.

## Anti-references

Generic SaaS landing pages: hero metrics (big number + small label), identical icon-card grids, stock illustration libraries, "Scale your business" or "10x your productivity" copy, feature-list fatigue.

Dark mode with neon accents: dark UI with neon purple or green glows, glassmorphism used as a default surface treatment, blockchain-adjacent or AI-hype aesthetics, aggressive purple-gradient-on-dark palettes.

## Design Principles

1. **Show capability, don't claim it.** The site's animations, technical components, and copy all demonstrate what Protolith Labs can build — they don't tell you how great it is.
2. **Earn attention, don't demand it.** Motion and color are deployed sparingly so that when they appear, they land. Restraint is the medium.
3. **Precision over decoration.** Every visual detail should be deliberate — from the dashed PlusBox borders to the corner markers. Ornament that doesn't communicate is noise.
4. **Calm confidence, not urgency.** No countdown timers, no "limited slots", no social proof walls. The tone is expert-to-peer, not vendor-to-prospect.
5. **Production-grade everything.** The same quality bar applied to client software applies to the site itself: performance, accessibility, no cut corners.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Respects `prefers-reduced-motion` throughout. No hard dependency on color alone to communicate meaning.
